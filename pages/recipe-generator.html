Food Recipe Generator

A web app that generates recipe ideas from whatever's in your kitchen. Filters for dietary restrictions and your preferences.

Role: Frontend + Integration Tools: React, Node.js, Express, Spoonacular API, Google Vision Team: 3-person hackathon team

Overview

The idea was simple: cooking shouldn't start with a grocery run. We built a recipe generator that takes whatever ingredients you already have, applies your dietary preferences, and returns recipes you can actually make tonight.

The app connected a React frontend to a Node.js backend powered by the Spoonacular API. We also experimented with Google Vision for image-based ingredient detection. Snap a photo of your fridge and let the app figure out what you're working with.

The Core Problem

A lot of effort goes into deciding what to cook. You check what you have, figure out what to make, and then realize you're missing half the ingredients. We wanted to shortcut that entire process by starting with what's already in your kitchen.

The goal was to make meal planning feel less like a chore and more like a suggestion from a friend who knows what's in your pantry.

Ingredient Input Recipe Matching Dietary Filters Image Scanning

How It Works

Step 1

Ingredients

Input what you have on hand, type it in or upload a photo for automatic detection via Google Vision.

Step 2

Preferences

Set dietary restrictions, taste preferences, and any ingredients you want to avoid.

Step 3

Matching

The backend queries Spoonacular's database and filters results based on your inputs and available ingredients.

Step 4

Results

Get a curated list of recipes you can actually make, complete with instructions and ingredient lists.

What I Took Away

This was one of those projects where the constraint (build it in a weekend) actually helped. We couldn't overthink the architecture or the design, we just had to make it work. The biggest lesson was around API integration. Spoonacular's data was messy in places, and learning to handle edge cases in recipe matching was a real exercise in defensive programming.

If I had more time, I'd improve the image detection accuracy, add a saved-recipes feature, and build out a more polished mobile experience.
