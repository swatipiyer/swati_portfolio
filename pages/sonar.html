Design Sprint · UC Irvine · 2024

Sonar

Product Designer, True To You Design-A-Thon

An anonymous music-sharing platform designed to connect people across cultures through the music they love, without the social pressure of public profiles.

FigmaRapid PrototypingDesign Sprint
The Prompt

The design-a-thon theme was "True To You," asking teams to create something that lets people express who they really are. Most social platforms reward curation over honesty. We wanted to flip that by designing around music taste as identity, stripped of names and photos.

What We Built

Sonar is an anonymous music-sharing app where users discover other people through what they listen to, not what they look like. You share tracks, find overlaps with strangers, and connect through shared musical discovery. The prototype focused on the core loop: share, match, explore.

What I took away

This was a fast build. The interesting constraint was designing a social experience where anonymity is the feature, not the bug. Every screen had to feel personal without revealing the person behind it.

It was a good exercise in scoping under pressure and making design decisions that serve the concept instead of defaulting to what social apps usually look like.
