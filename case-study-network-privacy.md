# Network Traffic & Privacy
**UC Davis Department of Computer Science · Research Assistant · Jul 2023 – Jan 2026**

---

## The Problem

The lab was collecting network traffic data from 500+ users for security research — but had no reliable pipeline to anonymize it before analysis. Data sat in raw form longer than it should have, and the manual process was slow enough that it was holding up research timelines.

## What I Did

I built Python and SQL pipelines to automate the anonymization process end-to-end. On the security side, I benchmarked several intrusion detection systems using statistical methods to figure out which actually performed best under real conditions — not just on paper.

As part of the UR2PHD program, I also ran independent research that surfaced 3 previously undocumented security vulnerabilities, and wrote up mitigation approaches in a formal technical proposal. The findings eventually made their way into departmental security guidelines.

## Results

- **50% faster** research turnaround after pipeline automation
- **95% accuracy** on the intrusion detection benchmark
- Security recommendations adopted into department-wide guidelines
- Presented findings to faculty and cross-functional research teams
